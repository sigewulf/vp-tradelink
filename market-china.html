    Working With Chinese Buyers

    What Chinese Buyers Expect

    COA Before Price

    Chinese buyers — particularly for minerals and pharma-grade agro — always request COA before discussing price. Send your COA alongside your first quotation. A buyer who receives a detailed, NABL-certified analysis report immediately views you as a serious, organized supplier rather than a price-shopper.

    Price Negotiation Is Standard

    Chinese buyers negotiate aggressively on first contact. This is normal — it is not an insult and it is not final. Quote your price with a realistic margin buffer. Do not accept the first counter. Make one moderate concession, then hold. Chinese buyers respect suppliers who hold firm more than those who collapse quickly.

    FOB is Preferred

    Most Chinese trading companies prefer FOB because they have established freight relationships and control their own logistics cost. Quote FOB Mundra as your primary Incoterm for China. CIF available on request for buyers without a freight forwarder relationship.

    GACC Registration for Food

    For agro commodities going to China — sesame, cumin, psyllium, moringa — Chinese customs may require the Indian exporter or Indian facility to be registered with GACC (General Administration of Customs China). Confirm GACC requirements with your CHA before finalising the first Chinese shipment. This is a compliance requirement, not a commercial one, but failing it will delay or block your shipment at the Chinese port.
